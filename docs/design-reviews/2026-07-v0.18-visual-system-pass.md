# Design Review #1 — final UX/UI and visual-system pass

**Review:** v0.18 pre-field (Design Review #1) — specialist visual/interaction pass and synthesis input
**Inspected:** `main` at `a698cd7` (identical to the deployed production build — deploy run 29191196734 succeeded for this commit)
**Method:** token/source audit of `src/styles/global.css`, `src/map/mapStyle.ts` and screen components; computed WCAG contrast ratios and HSL hue analysis; Playwright screenshots of all six destinations at 390 × 844, 1024 × 768 and 1440 × 900 plus focused crops; in-context colour-candidate renders.
**Rule observed:** no product code was changed by this pass; no issues were created.
**Triage outcome:** the owner triaged the Decision shortlist on 2026-07-12 — D1/D2 accepted (implemented in the closure PR), D3 needs phone evidence, D4/D7 deferred, D5/D6/D8 accepted as documentation (docs/VISUAL-DESIGN-AUTHORITY.md). Decisions of record live in the main review report.

## Executive summary

**Strongest aspects.** The app has a genuinely coherent Nordic material system: one surface family (stone `#dce4d8` → paper `#e6ede3` → paper-bright `#eef3ec`), one ink ramp, three brand hues (spruce, cloudberry, glacier) used with consistent intent, and a card language (16px padding, 14px stack rhythm, `--r-md` radius) that repeats across Today, Stages, Stops, Lists and Settings. After the Today stage-block redesign, the control language is at its clearest yet: **solid fill = tap me** (cloudberry primary, glacier secondary, spruce tab/segment states), **quiet outline or glass = metadata**. Cross-device coherence is strong — the same product is unmistakable at 390, 1024 and 1440.

**Most visible inconsistencies.** (1) External-link anchors render in **default browser blue** — the only truly foreign colour in the product, visible on the Stops detail card and every external source link, caused by a missing global `a` rule. (2) The **completed-journey colour is a shared `--good` token** whose hue (108°) sits 43° warmer than the spruce family (151°) — the owner's instinct is measurably right, though the effect at 34px dot size is subtle; changing it globally would drag packing, checkboxes, meters and readiness icons with it. (3) Several **small tinted-fill text pairings sit below 4.5:1** (white on cloudberry 3.28:1, white on glacier 3.59:1, ink-faint legends ~3.0:1 against paper) — an outdoor-sunlight question more than a desk one. Everything else found is low-grade token drift (a handful of raw radii, fragmented micro-gaps and half-pixel font sizes), not visible disorder.

**Overall visual-system confidence: High.** The system is real and consistently applied; the findings below are refinements, not repairs. Nothing observed undermines field trust or task completion.

**Same role, different colours:** success is split between `--good #5d7a56` (fills) and `#46603f`-on-`#dfe9db` (status text pills) — related but unharmonised greens. **Different roles, similar colours:** none problematic; the closest pair (spruce vs ink) is intentional tonal layering.

### The completed-journey colour (owner concern)

Measured: `--good #5d7a56` = **HSL 108°, 17%, 41%** — a yellow-leaning moss green. The dark brand greens sit at 145–153° (spruce 151°, ink 145°); glacier at 191°. So the completed dots are **43° warmer than every dark green around them** — the owner's perception is objectively verifiable, *with one nuance*: the light surfaces themselves (paper 102°, stone 100°) are also warm-green, so `--good` harmonises with the background family while standing apart from the spruce/ink family it sits directly beside (dots 1–3 next to spruce-dark "Journey" heading and the cloudberry current dot).

Assessment of the four hypotheses:

- **A. Appropriate moss-green success state?** Partly — as a *packing/checklist* success colour on paper surfaces it works and harmonises with the surface hue.
- **B. Visually disconnected on Today?** Mildly but measurably yes — in-context crops show the current dots reading slightly olive next to the spruce hero; the effect at 34px is subtle, not jarring.
- **C. Would a cooler spruce-derived journey colour improve cohesion?** Yes — candidates at hue 151° visibly sit in the same family as the hero and header ink (see candidate crops), at the cost of reading less like a generic "success" green.
- **D. Would changing `--good` globally have side effects?** **Confirmed.** `--good` styles: journey-past dots, `.pack-status.is-packed`, checkbox fills, `.meter-fill`, readiness ✓ icons. A global change silently recolours Lists and Settings. Any journey-specific change requires a **dedicated token** (e.g. `--journey-complete`), leaving `--good` untouched.

### Colour directions for completed journey stages

| Direction | Hex | HSL | vs white numeral | vs glass surface (`#f6f8f3`) | Relationship | Advantages | Risks |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **J-0 Keep current** | `#5d7a56` | 108°,17%,41% | **4.79:1** ✓ | 4.48:1 ✓ | matches *surface* hue family; 43° from spruce | zero work; unified "done" colour app-wide | the measured warm drift remains; journey ≠ brand greens |
| **J-1 Spruce-tint (recommended candidate)** | `#4c6b5c` | 151°,17%,36% | **5.89:1** ✓ | 5.51:1 ✓ | exact spruce hue, lightened; equidistant from glacier | visibly same family as hero/ink; better numeral contrast; still clearly "filled/complete" vs outline future dots | needs new token + docs; slightly less "success-green", more "brand-green" |
| **J-2 Deep spruce** | `#3f5c4e` | 151°,19%,30% | **7.36:1** ✓ | 6.89:1 ✓ | closest to spruce itself | strongest cohesion + best contrast | dark enough to compete with the cloudberry *current* dot for visual weight; past days shouldn't outrank today |
| *(considered, not proposed)* glacier-slate `#55707a` | 196° | | 5.27:1 | 4.93:1 | glacier family | | wrong semantics — glacier already means "ready/spatial/technical", not "completed"; would collide with pack **Ready** |

All candidates keep white numerals ≥ 4.5:1. **No change is implemented**; J-1 vs J-0 is a genuine taste call that is honestly hard to judge at 34px — see Decision shortlist.

## 2. Spacing and layout-rhythm audit

Census of `global.css` (counts = rule occurrences):

- **Macro rhythm — coherent.** Screen padding `16px` (+safe-area), card padding `16px`, stack/card gaps `14px`, screen-head margin `18px`, hero/journey internal blocks `12–14px`. This 14/16/18 rhythm repeats on every screen and is the reason the app feels calm. No action needed.
- **Micro gaps — fragmented but mostly purposeful.** gap: 8 (×17), 6 (×15), 10 (×12), 4 (×5), 3 (×5), 14 (×6), 12 (×2), 5 (×3), 7 (×2), 1 (×1). The 3/4/6/8/10 steps map to real icon-text/chip/row purposes; the outliers are `7px` (map popup internals) and `5px` (chip gap at ≤360px, deliberate) and a lone `1px`.
- **Should a formal scale be adopted?** A strict 4/8/12/16/24/32 scale would *not* fit this design — its personality lives in the 6/10/14 half-steps. The honest formalisation is to **document the de-facto scale {2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 24}** and treat 7px/1px/odd values as migration targets when files are next touched.

| Finding | Components | Perceptible harm? | Direction | Migration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 7px gaps | map stop-popup internals | No — invisible in situ | fold to 6 or 8 | opportunistic |
| `margin-top` 12 vs 14 mixing | intra-card blocks vs inter-card | Borderline — both appear within cards in a few places | prefer 12 inside cards, 14 between cards; document | gradual |
| screen-head 18px vs stack 14px | every screen | No — deliberate hierarchy break | keep; document | none |

No spacing finding is user-visible enough to justify an immediate sweep.

## 3. Component-consistency audit

| Component | Radius | Fill/border | Type | Min-height | States |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| `.btn` (standard) | `--r-sm` 10 | paper + line; primary = spruce fill | 15px/600 | 46px | active scale, focus-visible ✓ |
| `.hero-action` (Today) | pill 999 | **solid** glacier / cloudberry | 13px/700 | 40px | active scale, focus-visible ✓ |
| Map `.chip` (selector) | pill | paper + line, `aria-pressed` spruce | 14px | 40px | pressed state ✓ |
| `.seg-btn` / filters | pill | paper-bright active / spruce active | 13px | ~36px | ✓ |
| `.stage-set-pill` (interactive) | pill | **transparent + spruce border** | 12.5px/700 | ~34px | hover/active |
| `.pill` metadata (Stages/Stops stats) | pill | paper-2 + line, ink-soft | 12px/600 | ~26px | none (correct) |
| `.hero-chip` metadata | pill | white-glass 10% | 12px/600 | ~29px | none (correct) |
| Journey dots | circle | good/cloudberry fill, line ring future | tnum 13px | 34px (28 ≤340px) | active scale, aria-current ✓ |
| Accordion headers (Stops/Stages guide) | — | transparent, chevron | 14–15px/700 | ≥44px | aria-expanded ✓ |
| Tab pills | `--r-sm`-ish | paper-2 active | 11px label + 22px icon | 64px bar | aria-current ✓ |

**Judgement.** After PR #46, the *fill logic* is the system: solid fill (spruce/cloudberry/glacier) = interactive commitment; paper + line = tappable but calm (map chips, standard buttons); glass/paper-2 quiet pill = inert metadata. That convention **should be written down and reused** — it is currently only implicit.

Two genuine deviations:

1. **`.stage-set-pill` is the weakest affordance in the app** — an *interactive* transparent pill differentiated from the *inert* stat pills on the same card only by border colour (spruce vs line) and label verb. It works (verb + placement carry it) but it is the one control that violates the otherwise clean fill-logic. Options: tint its fill (paper-bright), or accept as-is given "Set as current" is self-describing.
2. **Interactive shape split**: rectangular `--r-sm` buttons (Settings, forms, map action row) vs pill buttons (hero, selectors). This is *contextual variation that reads fine* — rectangular = workhorse forms, pill = inline/floating controls. Recommend documenting, not homogenising.

Touch targets: standard buttons 46px, hero actions/map chips 40px, journey dots 34px visual within a taller padded hit area, accordion headers ≥44px — acceptable; nothing under ~34px effective. Icon system: lucide sizes cluster on 13/15/16/18 with strokes 1.8 (decorative/facility) and 2–2.2 (UI emphasis) — consistent enough; `strokeWidth 2.4/2.1/1.5` appear once each (drift, trivial).

## 4. Typography and iconography audit

Hierarchy: eyebrow 12px/600/caps+tracking → h1 26px/700 (Today 30px hero title 23px) → card titles 15–16.5px/700 → body 14px → support 12.5–13.5px → micro-labels 9.5–11.5px/700/caps. The hierarchy itself is disciplined and repeats correctly across screens.

**Meaningful drift is confined to one band:** 12 / 12.5 / 13 / 13.5 / 14 / 14.5 all exist (61 rules), plus half-pixel micro sizes (9.5, 11.5). None of these is *visibly* wrong in situ; the cost is maintenance, not perception. Direction: document a nominal ladder (11 / 12 / 13 / 14 / 15 / 16 / 18 / 22 / 26 / 30) and fold the 0.5s in opportunistically. Not worth a dedicated migration.

Numerals: `tnum` (tabular) is applied consistently to stats, dots and meters — a quiet strength worth keeping fenced.

## 5. Nordic brand assessment

- **Still grounded?** Yes, firmly. The stone/paper surface family, spruce anchor, restrained cloudberry warmth and glacier cool read as one landscape-derived system; the contour-line background on Today and the hero's elevation silhouette tie the interface to the terrain material directly.
- **What reinforces it:** the surface ramp; the spruce hero and active states; cloudberry used *sparingly* as "now"; the muted sienna danger family (no alarm-red); system-font restraint; the terrain basemap palette (soft greens/greys) blending with the interface behind paper cards.
- **What feels foreign:** only the **default-blue anchors** (genuine breach) and — arguably — the Okabe-Ito **stage identification colours** on the Map. The latter is *deliberate semantic colour use* (colour-blind-safe stage identity, per the map-legibility iterations) and should stay, but it deserves an explicit one-line record as a sanctioned exception so future reviews don't re-litigate it. The `--good` warm-green is a family-tension inside the palette, not a foreign colour.
- **Map ↔ interface complementary?** Yes — the terrain palette is desaturated enough that paper cards and the spruce/cloudberry accents sit comfortably over it; the bright stage lines are the only saturated elements and they are the data.
- **Does Today feel like the same product as the rest?** Yes — more than before the stage-block redesign: the hero's cloudberry/glacier buttons now bridge to the map's chips and Settings' spruce primaries. The one place Today is *ahead* of the rest is exactly that fill-logic clarity; Stages' "Set as current" is the laggard.

## Findings catalogue

Severity/priority/evidence per `docs/DESIGN-REVIEW-FRAMEWORK.md`.

### DR-001 — Completed-journey colour shares the global `--good` token and drifts warm

**Lens:** Visual interface · Creator intent · **Area:** Today → Journey; token system
**Evidence:** Observed (token analysis, HSL measurement, in-context renders) · **Severity:** Low · **Priority:** P2 · **Confidence:** High

**Observation** `--good #5d7a56` (hue 108°) fills completed journey dots; every dark green around it is 145–153°. The same token drives packing "Packed", checkboxes, meter fills and readiness icons.
**Why it matters** Today is the brand anchor screen; its history row is the only place the warm green sits directly beside spruce surfaces. And the shared token makes the obvious fix (recolour) silently non-local.
**Proposed direction** Introduce a dedicated `--journey-complete` token. Candidate J-1 `#4c6b5c` (spruce hue, 5.89:1 on white numerals). `--good` stays untouched everywhere else.
**Alternative** J-0 keep as-is (defensible: harmonises with surface hue; subtle at 34px). J-2 `#3f5c4e` if maximum cohesion is wanted.
**Trade-off** J-1/J-2 make "done" on Today diverge from "done" on Lists — acceptable because the contexts never co-occur, but it splits the success semantics in the token file.
**Validation** Side-by-side on the actual phone outdoors (crops attached; the difference is small enough that desktop judgement is unreliable).

### DR-002 — External links render in default browser blue

**Lens:** Visual interface · First-time user · **Area:** Stops detail ("View official information"), Settings/Credits/Source links; `global.css`
**Evidence:** Observed (screenshot `crop-stop-open.png`; no `a` colour rule exists — `button { color: inherit }` has no anchor counterpart)
**Severity:** Medium · **Priority:** P1 · **Confidence:** High

**Observation** Anchors fall back to UA blue (`#0000ee`-class), the only colour outside the Nordic palette in the product, including inside `.btn`-styled anchors.
**Why it matters** Saturated default blue on the paper card reads as unfinished and breaks brand trust at exactly the moments the app asks users to verify official information.
**Proposed direction** Global `a { color: var(--glacier-700) }` (matching `.link-btn`) plus `a.btn { color: inherit }`; visited/focus states inherit the existing focus-visible system.
**Alternative** Per-instance classes only — worse; new anchors will regress.
**Trade-off** None meaningful; glacier-700 is 6.2:1 on paper.
**Validation** Re-screenshot Stops/Settings/Credits after change.

### DR-003 — Small tinted-fill text sits below 4.5:1 (outdoor question)

**Lens:** Accessibility & outdoor usability · **Area:** hero actions (white on cloudberry 3.28:1, white on glacier 3.59:1, both 13px/700), journey-future numerals & legends (`--ink-faint` ≈3.0:1 on paper at 11.5px), pack "Ready" (`#1f5b69` on glacier-soft — passes)
**Evidence:** Observed (computed ratios) · **Severity:** Medium · **Priority:** P2 · **Confidence:** High (measurement) / Low (real-world impact — needs field light)

**Observation** These pairings pre-date the redesign (the old hero CTA was the same cloudberry/white); the redesign added the glacier/white pairing at the same level.
**Why it matters** WCAG small-text AA wants 4.5:1; 13px/700 is technically small text. In alpine glare, marginal contrast degrades first.
**Proposed direction** Field-check legibility in sunlight during the owner's phone evaluation. If needed: darken fills one step (cloudberry ~`#a06f34`, glacier → `--glacier-700`) — both stay on-palette; bump ink-faint legends to ink-soft where they carry information.
**Alternative** Accept as established brand contrast (bold, short labels, generous letter spacing).
**Trade-off** Darker fills lose some of the current airiness; ink-soft legends de-emphasise less.
**Validation** Sunlight phone test; no desk-side change first.

### DR-004 — "Set as current" is the one control that breaks the fill-logic

**Lens:** Interaction & IA · Visual interface · **Area:** Stages cards
**Evidence:** Observed · **Severity:** Low · **Priority:** P3 · **Confidence:** Medium

**Observation** An interactive transparent pill sits on the same card as inert stat pills of identical shape, differentiated only by border colour and verb.
**Why it matters** The Today redesign established *solid = action, quiet = metadata*; this is the main remaining exception. The verb label mitigates in practice.
**Proposed direction** Give it a quiet solid surface (paper-bright fill, spruce text/border) — still subordinate to "Current", clearly not metadata.
**Alternative** Leave as-is; revisit only if the phone walkthrough shows hesitation.
**Trade-off** More visual weight on every non-current card (×6).
**Validation** Owner phone impression during Stages use.

### DR-005 — Success semantics use two unharmonised green pairs

**Lens:** Visual interface · **Area:** `--good` fills vs `#46603f`/`#dfe9db` status pills (tracking on-route, `.pill-good`)
**Evidence:** Observed · **Severity:** Low · **Priority:** P3 · **Confidence:** High

**Observation** Filled success uses `--good` (108°); text-on-tint success uses `#46603f` (107°) with its own tint — same family, never derived from each other, and neither relates to spruce.
**Why it matters** Harmless today; it becomes debt when a new success state is needed and there are two precedents. Resolution interacts with DR-001 (if `--journey-complete` is introduced, `--good` becomes packing/readiness-only and this pair can be documented as *fill vs tint* variants of one role).
**Proposed direction** Document the success family (fill token, tint pair) in the design-system header comment; no visual change.
**Alternative / Trade-off / Validation** None needed beyond the doc.

### DR-006 — Token-file drift: raw radii, micro-gap and half-pixel-type fragmentation

**Lens:** Systems & software integrity · Visual interface · **Area:** `global.css` (radii 8/10/12px literals in map popup/segments/facility chips; gaps 7px/1px; font sizes 9.5–14.5 half-steps)
**Evidence:** Observed (census) · **Severity:** Low · **Priority:** P3 · **Confidence:** High

**Observation** None of it is user-perceptible in situ; it is maintenance friction and slow entropy.
**Proposed direction** Add the de-facto scales (spacing {2–18,24}, radius {--r-sm/md/lg, pill}, type ladder, icon strokes 1.8/2/2.2) to the design-system comment block; migrate literals opportunistically when files are touched.
**Alternative** A one-shot normalisation PR — rejected: churn and regression risk without visible payoff.
**Trade-off** Living-with-drift until touched.
**Validation** None; documentation only.

### DR-007 — Map stage-identification palette is off-brand *by design* and should be recorded as such

**Lens:** Product strategy · Visual interface · **Area:** `STAGE_COLORS` (Okabe-Ito), map selector swatches
**Evidence:** Observed + prior iterations (map-legibility work) · **Severity:** Low (no defect) · **Priority:** No action beyond docs · **Confidence:** High

**Observation** Seven saturated, colour-blind-safe hues identify stages on terrain — intentionally outside the Nordic palette because identity coding on a muted basemap *requires* leaving it.
**Proposed direction** One sentence in the design-system header naming this a sanctioned semantic exception, so future brand passes don't flag it again.
