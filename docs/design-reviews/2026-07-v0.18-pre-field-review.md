### Summary

The review has been initiated against the accepted Design Authority framework.
The current production baseline is v0.17.1, while v0.18.0 is the active
milestone for beta trust and trail readiness. No release judgement is made at
initiation.

This review will test the complete experience across first-use comprehension,
realistic hiking workflows, interaction quality, visual consistency,
accessibility and outdoor usability, product focus, creator intent, and
systems/software integrity. It will explicitly distinguish observed evidence
from simulation and will not treat the simulated experienced-hiker lens as
field validation.

Creator Intent has been added as the ninth Design Authority lens through
[`ADR 0002`](../decisions/0002-add-creator-intent-lens.md). It keeps the intended
experience visible throughout the review and checks whether incremental changes
have preserved or weakened the product's defining decisions.

## Scope and evidence baseline

### In scope

- Today as the operational home screen.
- Map, including stage selection, route overview, Locate, foreground live
  tracking, off-route messaging, terrain/satellite layers and fullscreen use.
- Stops and route-specific hut/station information.
- Stage planning and elevation/statistics context.
- Packing and daily lists.
- Settings, Trail readiness, map downloads, install/update guidance,
  diagnostics, backup and restore.
- First installation, repeat use, session persistence, refresh/restart and
  update recovery.
- Offline preparation and degraded/offline behaviour.
- Phone-first field use, plus tablet and desktop preparation workflows.
- Accessibility and outdoor constraints: sunlight, gloves, fatigue,
  one-handed use, low attention and limited battery.
- Product-boundary discipline and the active v0.18.0 roadmap scope.
- Creator intent, including the intended Prepare → Walk → Arrive → Repeat rhythm
  and Today's role as the operational centre.
- Relevant state architecture, tests and operational failure modes.

### Out of scope

- Commercial viability, pricing or market sizing.
- Generalising the framework to other trails.
- Accounts, cloud sync or cross-device automation.
- New feature ideation unrelated to the current release objective.
- High-resolution Lantmäteriet orthophoto imagery while source access remains
  externally blocked.
- Claims of Kungsleden field validation before the September hike.

## Review plan

### Round 1 — Baseline and first-time walkthrough

1. Capture the live production build and supported viewport states.
2. Record install, update, offline-download and first-run behaviour.
3. Walk through Today, Map, Stops, Stages, Lists and Settings without relying on
   project history.
4. Record comprehension gaps, hidden dependencies and trust-signalling issues.

### Round 2 — Field simulation

Run and document the following scenarios:

1. Prepare at home on Wi-Fi.
2. Reopen in airplane mode.
3. Morning planning in a hut.
4. Midday position uncertainty in glare or rain.
5. Low-battery use with limited attention.
6. Arrival at a hut while fatigued.
7. App restart, update, partial download and recovery.

### Round 3 — Specialist passes

Conduct separate passes for:

- interaction and information architecture;
- visual interface consistency;
- accessibility and outdoor usability;
- product strategy and scope discipline;
- systems and software integrity;
- creator intent and preservation of defining design decisions.

### Round 4 — Synthesis and triage

- consolidate duplicate symptoms into systemic findings;
- assign evidence class, severity, priority and confidence;
- identify any conflict between creator intent and user, accessibility, safety
  or field evidence;
- review recommendations with Omar;
- record accepted, revised, deferred, rejected and evidence-needed decisions;
- create GitHub issues only for accepted work;
- publish the final release judgement and explicit limitations.

### Creator intent

**Governing question**  
Does the current implementation still fulfil the intended product experience,
and have incremental changes preserved or weakened the decisions that define
Fjällkompis?

**Checks**

- Does Today still operate as the centre of the hiking day rather than merely
  one screen among equals?
- Does the experience support the Prepare → Walk → Arrive → Repeat rhythm?
- Does Fjällkompis still reduce cognitive load rather than accumulate features?
- Does it remain a bounded offline hiking companion instead of drifting toward
  a generic mapping or outdoor platform?
- Do route-specific usefulness, offline trust and field clarity remain dominant?
- Have later changes contradicted or weakened earlier accepted decisions?
- Are any recommended changes genuine improvements, or do they alter the
  original direction and therefore require an explicit owner decision?
