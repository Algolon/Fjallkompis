## Executive summary

### Release judgement

**Ready with explicit limitations.**

The judgement is contingent on the short owner phone session (Field
simulation, part 2) finding no blocker; merging the review-closure PR records
that confirmation. It is explicitly **not** "Field-validated" — the
Kungsleden itself has not been walked with this companion.

### Summary

Across nine lenses the companion is coherent, honest about its boundaries and
architecturally trustworthy offline. During the review, Today was redesigned
into a genuine operational hub (owner-approved focused direction, implemented
and validated as PRs #45/#46), and a full visual-system audit
([visual-system pass](2026-07-v0.18-visual-system-pass.md)) found a healthy,
consistently applied Nordic design system with a small set of refinements —
two accepted and implemented (dedicated journey-completion colour; on-palette
links), the rest documented, deferred or awaiting field light.

### Strongest qualities

- **Offline-first integrity is real, not aspirational** — committed vector
  basemap, integrity-pinned data archives, deterministic static content,
  and test fences around state, camera bounds and navigation (observed).
- **Today now answers the morning questions at a glance** — day, route,
  stats, need-to-know highlight chips, and two clearly-roled follow-up
  actions with progressive depth into Stages and Map (observed, validated).
- **One visual system across six screens and three device classes** — the
  surface ramp, ink ramp and three brand hues carry stable meanings; the
  control-vs-metadata fill convention is now explicit
  ([VISUAL-DESIGN-AUTHORITY](../VISUAL-DESIGN-AUTHORITY.md)) (observed,
  measured).
- **Scope discipline** — the anti-growth guardrail on the Today block is
  enforced by tests; archived features stay archived (observed).

### Most important risks

- **Field conditions are unproven** — sunlight, gloves, cold, battery and
  multi-day reliability have only bounded or simulated evidence (explicit
  limitation).
- **Marginal small-text contrast on tinted fills** (white on cloudberry
  3.3:1 / glacier 3.6:1 at bold 13px; faint 11.5px legends ~3:1) — fine at a
  desk, unproven in glare; under field observation (DR-003, Needs evidence).
- **Single-device, single-user evidence base** — no external users; the
  experienced-hiker lens remains simulated.

### Required before intended use

1. The short phone session below (cold start, airplane-mode reopen, Today
   navigation, D3 sunlight checklist) — gates the closure-PR merge.
2. Complete the already-planned v0.18.0 trail-readiness scope (offline
   downloads on the actual device; readiness checks green on the phone).
3. Nothing else from this review blocks intended personal use.

## Perspective reviews

### First-time user — *simulated, supported by observed IA*
A new user can tell what the product is (Today's summary + explicit offline
promise), where they are (day dots, current pill) and what to do next (Stage
Guide / View Route). The previously hidden day guides are now discoverable
from the home screen. Residual risk: comprehension is untested with real
outside users.

### Experienced hiker — *simulated*
The information set matches a real morning check: distance/climb/time,
exceptional characteristics (exposure, snow, high point), tonight's stop and
its gaps (no shop), with hedged seasonal facts (boats "possible", never
promised). Nothing found that manufactures false confidence; live tracking
stays opt-in and clearly beta.

### Field simulation — *part 1 observed/emulated; part 2 pending on the phone*
Emulated passes: offline-capable static content, airplane behaviour by
design (no network dependencies in core flows), stage switching, deep links,
map-state memory — all pass. The **owner phone session** below is the
review's real-device gate:

**Phone checklist (owner; gates the closure-PR merge)**

- [ ] **A. Cold start:** fully close installed app → reopen online → newest
      build activates (View Route is glacier-filled; completed journey dots
      are the new spruce-green) → note the running build.
- [ ] **B. Offline reopen:** open online → airplane mode → fully close →
      reopen → Today, chips, Stage Guide and Map load with the offline
      content expected for this installation (record honestly what is
      missing if downloads weren't done).
- [ ] **C. Navigation:** Stage Guide opens today's expanded guide scrolled
      into view; View Route focuses today's stage; away-and-back keeps the
      map view; a fresh Map visit shows the full route.
- [ ] **D. Sunlight (D3):** at battery-realistic brightness first, then max —
      classify each as Comfortable / Marginal / Insufficient: Stage Guide
      button · View Route button · cloudberry current dot · completed dots
      (`#4c6b5c`) · future dots · journey endpoint labels · faint
      metadata/legends · Today glass surfaces · primary Map controls.
      Record device, lighting condition and brightness setting.

One short session is *not* outdoor validation; it is the honest minimum for
a pre-field judgement.

### Interaction and information architecture — *observed*
Progressive depth holds everywhere: summary → authority screen → spatial
context. One-shot payload deep-links (Stops, Stages) behave identically.
The map's browse state is deliberately decoupled from the trip state and
fenced by tests. Weakest affordance: Stages' "Set as current" outline pill
(DR-004, deferred).

### Visual interface — *observed, measured*
See the [visual-system pass](2026-07-v0.18-visual-system-pass.md). One
perceptible defect (default-blue anchors — DR-002, fixed). The completed-day
colour drift was measurable (hue 108° vs spruce 151°) and resolved with a
dedicated `--journey-complete` token (DR-001). Remaining drift is minor,
documented, and governed by the new
[VISUAL-DESIGN-AUTHORITY](../VISUAL-DESIGN-AUTHORITY.md).

### Accessibility and outdoor usability — *observed; field pending*
Non-interactive metadata uses non-interactive semantics; disclosures and
accordions are ARIA-correct; focus-visible is systematic; text always
accompanies icons; long-press selection is suppressed outside editable
fields; touch targets ≥ 40px on primary actions. Open question: marginal
tinted-fill contrast in glare (DR-003 → phone checklist D).

### Product strategy — *observed*
The bounded offline-companion identity is intact and actively defended
(fixed-responsibility tests on the Today block; archived checklist stayed
archived; map palette exception documented rather than "fixed"). No
capability found that fails to earn its place.

### Systems and software integrity — *observed*
Static deterministic data modules with `.d.mts` typings and node-test
integrity suites; no GPS/network/clock dependencies added by the review's
changes; integrity-pinned map archives; CI (test/typecheck/build) plus
deploy verification on every main push. All tests green at closure.

### Creator intent — *observed against ADR 0002*
Today operates as the centre of the hiking day; cognitive load went down
(glanceable chips replacing nothing-at-a-glance); the product did not drift
toward a generic platform; earlier accepted decisions (map-state memory,
battery-conscious statics, no-unnecessary-scroll) were preserved and are now
partially test-enforced. The review found intent *strengthened*.

## Finding catalogue

Consolidated findings of record (full analysis in the

### DR-001 — Completed-journey colour shared `--good` and drifted warm
**Lens:** Visual interface · Creator intent · **Area:** Today Journey; tokens
· **Evidence:** Observed (measured) · **Severity:** Low · **Priority:** P2 ·
**Confidence:** High · **Decision:** **Accepted (D1) — implemented**
`--good` (`#5d7a56`, hue 108°) sat 43° warmer than the spruce family beside
it and was shared with packing/checks/meters, making recolouring non-local.
→ Dedicated `--journey-complete: #4c6b5c` (spruce hue; white numerals
5.9:1), journey dots only; `--good` untouched elsewhere. Trade-off: "done"
on Today now differs from "done" on Lists (the contexts never co-occur).
Validation: computed contrast, in-context renders, phone checklist D.

### DR-002 — External links rendered default browser blue
**Lens:** Visual interface · First-time user · **Area:** Stops/Settings/
Credits anchors · **Evidence:** Observed · **Severity:** Medium ·
**Priority:** P1 · **Confidence:** High · **Decision:** **Accepted (D2) —
implemented**
No global `a` rule existed; anchors (including `a.btn`) fell back to UA blue
— the only off-palette colour in the product. → Global on-palette link
styling (glacier-700 + underline + visited/hover/active/focus-visible
states), explicit `.btn` ink so button-anchors follow button styling,
MapLibre attribution shielded. Fenced by `tests/design-system.test.mjs`.
Trade-off: none meaningful (6.2:1 on paper).

### DR-003 — Marginal small-text contrast on tinted fills
**Lens:** Accessibility & outdoor usability · **Area:** hero action text
(3.28/3.59:1 white on cloudberry/glacier at bold 13px), `--ink-faint`
legends (~3:1) · **Evidence:** Observed (measured); field impact
Unvalidated hypothesis · **Severity:** Medium · **Priority:** P2 ·
**Confidence:** High (numbers) / Low (field impact) · **Decision:**
**Needs evidence (D3)** — phone checklist D. If insufficient in real light:
darken fills one step / lift legends to ink-soft. No speculative change now.

## Cross-review synthesis

The review's separate threads point the same way: the product's strength is
*coherence under constraint* — offline honesty, bounded scope, one visual
language — and its residual risk is entirely *unproven field conditions*.
Nothing found suggests structural rework; everything accepted was refinement
of an already-consistent system.

## What surprised us?

- **Today, not Map, emerged as the operational centre.** The review began
  assuming Map was the heart of the product; the stage-block work showed the
  morning decision loop lives on Today, with Map as spatial depth.
- **The visual system was stronger than assumed.** The audit expected
  accumulated drift; it found one genuine defect (default-blue links) and a
  system disciplined enough that the audit's main output was *writing the
  rules down* rather than fixing violations.
- **The completed-day olive shift was measurable but minor.** The owner's
  eye was validated by hue mathematics (43° warm of spruce) — yet the fix
  mattered more for token hygiene (decoupling journey from `--good`) than
  for the pixels themselves.
- **The map palette earned its exception.** What first looked like brand
  inconsistency is a deliberate, colour-blind-safe identification system —
  now documented so it is never "fixed" by mistake.
- **The most valuable improvements were trust, clarity and consistency** —
  not new features. The review added almost no capability; it made existing
  capability legible and dependable.

## Final recommendation

**Ready with explicit limitations** — Fjällkompis is acceptable for the
owner's intended personal September use as a *companion alongside proper
navigation tools*, once the short phone session confirms cold start, offline
reopen, Today navigation and no sunlight blocker (merging the closure PR
records this confirmation).

**Remaining for September field validation (outside this review):**
multi-day battery behaviour, sunlight/rain/glove operation, GPS behaviour on
the actual trail, hut-morning workflow reality, and offline map sufficiency
across the full route.

**Follow-up review triggers:**

1. **Pre-departure focused check** — after the v0.18.0 readiness scope and
   real-device download/readiness checks are complete (lightweight, focused
   review).
2. **Design Review #2 — Kungsleden field review** — after the September
   hike, converting field notes into the first externally validated review.

## Explicit limitations

- No actual Kungsleden field validation yet; this review cannot and does not
  claim it.
- Sunlight evidence is limited to one short phone session (checklist above);
  glare, rain and glove/cold-hand operation remain effectively untested.
- Battery evidence is limited to design-level review (static content, no
  background GPS); no multi-day power data exists.
- GPS mechanics were validated only in the bounded Delft pilot, not across
  the full trail.
- High-resolution Lantmäteriet orthophoto imagery remains externally
  blocked; satellite imagery is the Sentinel-2 fallback.
- The experienced-hiker and first-time-user lenses are simulated; no
  external users participated.
- AI-assisted analysis informed findings; evidence classes are recorded so
  simulation is never mistaken for field proof.
